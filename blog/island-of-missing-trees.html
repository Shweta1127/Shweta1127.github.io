Island of missing trees

Books

Summary

Elif Shafak’s The Island of Missing Trees feels less like a book and more like a soft, rhythmic heartbeat.

It is a story that wanders through the sun-drenched streets of a divided Cyprus, following Kostas and Defne—two beautiful souls who dare to love in a world determined to keep them apart. To me, it’s a gentle reminder that love is the most natural thing in the world, even when humans try to make it complicated.

The most magical part? A wise, soulful fig tree narrates much of the journey. I find so much comfort in her voice; she reminds us that nature watches over us, holding our secrets in her rings and breathing through our silences. When the story travels to London, we meet young Ada, who is trying to find her place in the world. She discovers that while grief can feel like a heavy winter, our roots have a way of reaching deep into the earth to find hidden springs of hope.

This book is a soft kiss to anyone who has ever felt uprooted. It teaches us that nothing is ever truly gone—it just changes form. It’s a gorgeous, optimistic tapestry that proves even the deepest wounds can be mended by time, storytelling, and a little bit of green magic

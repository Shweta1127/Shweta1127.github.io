The Fault in Our Stars

by John Green

✍ Shweta Suryavanshi 📅 June 12, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read
Author John Green
Language English
Published 2012
Genre Romance / YA
Setting USA
Themes Love, Illness, Mortality, Meaning

Summary

The Fault in Our Stars by John Green is a heart-wrenching love story about two teenagers, Hazel and Augustus, who meet at a cancer support group. Hazel, a 16-year-old girl with thyroid cancer, and Augustus, a 17-year-old boy who lost his leg to bone cancer, embark on a journey of self-discovery and love. Despite their illnesses, they find solace and comfort in each other's company. Their story is a beautiful and poignant portrayal of the human experience.

As Hazel and Augustus navigate their relationship, they are faced with the harsh realities of their mortality. They grapple with the meaning of life and the impermanence of everything. Their love becomes a beacon of hope and strength, allowing them to confront their fears and find joy in the present moment. Through their story, Green explores the complexities of life, love, and loss, raising important questions about the human condition.

The Bitter Beauty of Mortality

The novel delves into the theme of mortality, highlighting the cruel irony of life's fragility. Hazel and Augustus's experiences serve as a reminder that life is precious and fleeting, and that every moment should be cherished. Green's portrayal of their struggles and triumphs is both heartbreaking and uplifting, encouraging readers to reevaluate their own priorities and values.

The characters' awareness of their own mortality adds a layer of depth and complexity to the story. It forces them to confront the impermanence of everything, including their love for each other. This awareness also prompts them to seek answers to life's biggest questions, leading to a profound exploration of the human experience. Through their journey, Green shows that even in the face of adversity, hope, love, and meaning can be found.

"Some infinities are bigger than other infinities." — The Fault in Our Stars, John Green

What Lingers

Reading The Fault in Our Stars was a deeply emotional experience for me. The novel's thought-provoking themes and well-developed characters resonated deeply, leaving me feeling both sad and hopeful. The story's ability to balance humor and pathos is a testament to Green's masterful storytelling, making it a truly unforgettable read.

This book will stay with me for a long time, not just because of its beautiful prose and memorable characters, but also because of the important questions it raises about life, love, and mortality. The Fault in Our Stars is a powerful reminder to appreciate the present moment and to cherish the people and experiences that make life worth living. It's a novel that will continue to resonate with readers, inspiring them to reflect on their own values and priorities.

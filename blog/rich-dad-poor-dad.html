Rich Dad Poor Dad

by Robert Kiyosaki

✍ Shweta Suryavanshi 📅 June 12, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read
Author Robert Kiyosaki
Language English
Published 1997
Genre Finance
Setting USA
Themes Money, Investing, Mindset

Summary

Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki is a personal finance book that tells the story of the author's two fathers, one rich and one poor, and the lessons he learned from them about money and investing. The rich dad, Robert's best friend's father, is a successful businessman who teaches Robert about the importance of financial independence and building wealth. The poor dad, Robert's own father, is a well-educated but financially struggling man who teaches Robert about the importance of hard work and education. Through their contrasting approaches to money, Robert learns valuable lessons about financial literacy and freedom.

The central conflict of the book is Robert's struggle to reconcile the different values and financial strategies of his two fathers, and to find his own path to financial independence. Through a series of stories and anecdotes, Robert illustrates the ways in which the rich dad's approach to money and investing ultimately led to greater financial freedom and security. The book follows Robert's journey as he applies the lessons he learned from his rich dad to his own life, and achieves financial success as a result.

The Power of Financial Education

One of the key themes of Rich Dad Poor Dad is the importance of financial education and literacy. Robert argues that the traditional education system fails to provide people with the knowledge and skills they need to manage their finances effectively, and that this lack of knowledge can lead to financial struggle and insecurity. He advocates for a more proactive and informed approach to money management, and provides readers with practical tips and strategies for achieving financial freedom.

The book also emphasizes the importance of taking control of one's own financial destiny, rather than relying on others or waiting for circumstances to change. Robert encourages readers to think critically about their financial assumptions and to challenge the status quo, rather than simply accepting the conventional wisdom about money and investing. By doing so, readers can begin to build wealth and achieve financial independence, rather than simply getting by from paycheck to paycheck.

"It's not how much money you make, but how much money you keep, how hard it works for you, and how many generations you keep it for." — Rich Dad Poor Dad, Robert Kiyosaki

What Lingers

Reading Rich Dad Poor Dad was a transformative experience for me, as it challenged many of my assumptions about money and investing. The book's emphasis on financial education and literacy resonated deeply with me, and I found myself reflecting on my own financial habits and strategies. The book's message of empowerment and self-reliance was also inspiring, and it motivated me to take a more active and informed approach to managing my finances.

I think Rich Dad Poor Dad will stay with me for a long time, as its lessons and themes continue to resonate with me. The book's emphasis on the importance of financial freedom and independence has had a lasting impact on my approach to money and investing, and I have found myself applying its principles in my own life. I would highly recommend the book to anyone looking to improve their financial literacy and achieve greater financial security and freedom.

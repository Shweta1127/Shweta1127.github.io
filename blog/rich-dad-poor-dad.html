Rich Dad Poor Dad

by Robert Kiyosaki

✍ Shweta Suryavanshi 📅 June 16, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read
Author Robert Kiyosaki
Language English
Published 1997
Genre Finance
Setting USA
Themes Money, Investing, Mindset

Summary

Rich Dad Poor Dad is a personal finance book written by Robert Kiyosaki, which tells the story of his two fathers: his own 'poor dad', who was a well-educated but financially struggling government worker, and his best friend's 'rich dad', a high school dropout who became a successful businessman. The book explores the different mindsets and financial strategies of the two fathers. Through his experiences, Kiyosaki learned valuable lessons about money, investing, and the importance of financial education. The book is a reflection of Kiyosaki's journey towards financial independence.

The central conflict of the book revolves around the different approaches to money and wealth creation. Kiyosaki's 'poor dad' believed in the traditional approach of working hard, saving money, and investing in a secure job, while his 'rich dad' took a more unconventional approach, focusing on building wealth through investments, real estate, and entrepreneurship. As Kiyosaki navigates his way through the two different philosophies, he begins to question the traditional views on money and wealth. The story arc of the book is a personal and financial journey of self-discovery and growth.

The Power of Financial Education

One of the key themes of the book is the importance of financial education. Kiyosaki's 'rich dad' taught him that financial literacy is essential for achieving financial independence. He learned about the different types of income, such as earned income, passive income, and portfolio income, and how to create wealth through smart investments. The book emphasizes the need for individuals to take control of their financial education and not rely solely on traditional schooling.

Kiyosaki's 'rich dad' also taught him about the importance of taking calculated risks and being open to new opportunities. He learned that financial freedom requires a willingness to challenge conventional wisdom and think outside the box. The book encourages readers to adopt a similar mindset and to be proactive in their pursuit of financial knowledge and wealth creation. By doing so, individuals can break free from the constraints of a limited financial mindset and achieve their financial goals.

"It's not how much money you make, but how much money you keep, how hard it works for you, and how many generations you keep it for." — Rich Dad Poor Dad, Robert Kiyosaki

What Lingers

Reading Rich Dad Poor Dad was a life-changing experience for me. The book made me realize the importance of financial education and the need to take control of my own financial journey. It challenged my traditional views on money and wealth, and it encouraged me to think differently about my financial goals. The book's message of financial freedom and independence resonated deeply with me, and it inspired me to take action towards achieving my own financial goals.

This book will stay with me for a long time, as it has had a profound impact on my perspective on money and wealth. The lessons I learned from Kiyosaki's experiences will continue to guide me as I navigate my own financial journey. I believe that the book's message of financial education and empowerment is essential for anyone looking to achieve financial freedom, and I highly recommend it to anyone who is willing to challenge their conventional views on money and wealth.
